Problem 5. Affective and exploratory visualizations

Skills you will demonstrate

In this problem, you will demonstrate your ability to communicate about your visualization and give feedback to others. You will also demonstrate your ability to design and execute an appropriate statistical analysis for your data.

Problem

a. Sharing your affective visualization

This is a component you will complete in workshop on Thursday 6 June. We will be taking attendance that day.

You will present your affective visualization and give feedback to 2-3 other classmates in class.

b. Comparing visualizations

In 5-10 sentences, compare and contrast your affective visualization from Homework 3/workshop, the exploratory visualization you made for Homework 2, and the communicative/exploratory visualization you made for the midterm.

How are the visualizations different from each other? What similarities do you see between all your visualizations?

Problem 6. Data analysis

In this part of the final, you will analyze your data using the response and predictor variable(s) you have measured.

a. Response and predictor variables

List your response and predictor variable(s) and describe what kind of data (for example: continuous, categorical, ordinal, binary, etc.) each variable is.

b. Articulate a question

Write your “research question”. For example, “what is the effect of ____ on ____?” or “what are the differences in ____ on ____?”

c. Describe what kind of test or model you would use

Describe why that test or model would be appropriate given your response and predictor variables.

Note

You have to use one of the tests/models we learned about in this class. Make sure your response variables are appropriate for those tests!

d. Follow-ups

Describe what kind of effect size, post-hoc, correlation coefficient, prediction calculation, or any other follow-up you would do and why.

e. Analyze your data

Include the code and output for all parts: assumption checks/diagnostics, the code itself, follow-up tests.

f. Visualize your results

Making sure that your visualization matches up with the test you ran in part e, visualize your results with the underlying data and 95% confidence intervals and/or standard error.

Finalize your figure, get rid of visual clutter, and use colors/shapes/other aesthetics as appropriate. You will be graded on the aesthetic presentation of your analysis and how well it matches up with the statistical test you ran in part e.

g. Write a caption for your visualization.

h. Write a methods section.

Describe
- what variables you collected
- how you collected your data

i. Write a results section.

Describe
- what you found (with the appropriate summary of the statistical tests)
- your interpretation of your results

Turn in your raw data as a .csv along with your final.

Problem 7. Statistical critique

a. Find the methods

Revisit the methods of the paper you chose. If there are multiple methods, choose one that is in the list of methods we cover in this class:

  • t-test
  • Analysis of variance (ANOVA)
  • Mann-Whitney U
  • Kruskal-Wallis
  • Wilcoxon rank sum
  • Linear model or linear regression
  • Spearman correlation
  • Pearson correlation
  • logistic regression
  • Generalized linear mixed effect model

Paste the text of the methods into your document.

b. Method description

What is the response variable and what is the predictor? If a mixed effect model, what are the random effects?

c. Assumption

What assumptions have to be met for using this method? If a generalized linear mixed effect model, what type of error distribution is appropriate for the response variable?

d. Critique

Was this an appropriate method for the authors to use? Why or why not? If not, what would be a more appropriate method to use and why?
